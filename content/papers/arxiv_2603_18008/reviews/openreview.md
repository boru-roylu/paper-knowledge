## WmDSjIhg71

- summary: This paper presents TherapyGym, a framework for evaluating the fidelity and safety of LLM-based therapist. They developed 11 aspects for fidelity based on Cognitive Therapy Rating Scale (CTRS), as well as 4 tags related to safety. They collect and release TherapyJudgeBench, 116 dialogues (each dialogues contain 10 turns) and 1,270 expert ratings. Based on the annotated data, they build FidelitySafetyJudge, a model for evaluating clinical dialogues, and finally fine-tunes therapist chatbot LLM, applying GRPO to improve scores from FidelitySafetyJudge. The new dataset, annotated by real experts, can be useful for the community, as well as evaluation framework grounded in clinical practices. The reviewers (and AC as well) acknowledge the value of dataset. The paper is written pretty clearly and well-motivated. Reviewer RSRq wanted a clearer contextualization of work compared to prior work. I agree that current Table 1 is not very informative (and I think the author response partially addressed this concern). Another major concern is the validity of the synthetic conversation dataset (i.e., how realistic are the simulated conversation?) The authors defend their choice by relying on prior work which showed promising simulation ability of LLMs, but independent validation study would strengthen the paper. Multiple reviewers also pointed out missing details about data collection and implementation. The reviewer also raised the potential concern of reward hacking. I am not sure fine-tuning with GRPO (section 2.3.2) adds meaningfully to the paper -- it is not novel technique, and the improved results are sensible but not surprising. The paper might benefit from a bit more thorough documentation and validation of dataset rather than fine-tuning experiments. Overall, the paper studies a well-motivated problem and proposes a potentially useful dataset for the research community yet has some concerns that need to be addressed.
